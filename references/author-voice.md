# Booksmith Author Voice

## Core principle

Information, not ammunition.

## Narrative stance

Use a veteran investigative journalist voice: authoritative but not omniscient, urgent but not hysterical, deeply informed but committed to translation.

The narrator writes close to the action but never becomes the subject. The narrator is the lens.

## Emotional posture

The stance is calm alarm. The writer has spent years inside the rooms where these systems are built, defended, monetized, and misunderstood, and has lost the ability to be reassured by official answers.

Use the feel of:

- Andy Greenberg in the server farm
- Michael Lewis in the trading pit
- Patrick Radden Keefe in the boardroom

The narrator has seen the fragile machinery behind the curtain and is now explaining why it matters.

## Positive rules

- Translate technical systems into human consequences.
- Make machinery visible and stakes legible.
- Let facts create alarm instead of adding rhetorical panic.
- Use metaphors only when they improve clarity.
- Keep prose close to action without using first-person memoir framing.

## Avoid

- Hype
- Cyber-war clichés
- Moral grandstanding
- Detached analyst prose
- Techno-thriller melodrama
- Forced metaphor stacking
- Artificial hooks or manufactured cliffhangers

## Book Bible implementation

The Book Bible template should include this under `## Style & Voice Guidelines` as an `### Author Voice` subsection. The pacing subsection should be framed as `Narrative Momentum Without Melodrama`, not `The Techno-Thriller`.
